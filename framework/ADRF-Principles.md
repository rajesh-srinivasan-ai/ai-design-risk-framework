# ADRF Principles v0.1

## Purpose

The AI Design Risk Framework (ADRF) provides a structured approach for identifying, discussing, and classifying design risks in AI systems.

As AI systems continue to evolve, recurring design risks can emerge across products, platforms, and organizations. ADRF exists to establish a common language for recognizing these patterns before they become normalized or difficult to address.

ADRF is not intended to evaluate products, compare vendors, or track implementation defects. Its focus is on recurring design risk patterns and their potential implications.

---

# ADRF Principles

## ADRF-P1 — Risk Orientation

ADRF focuses on design risks and their potential consequences rather than implementation defects, bugs, or operational incidents.

A candidate ADRF risk should describe a risk pattern, not a product malfunction.

---

## ADRF-P2 — Product Neutrality

ADRF identifies recurring design risk patterns rather than product-specific behaviors.

Products, platforms, and vendors may be referenced as examples of a pattern, but they are not the subject of the framework.

The focus of ADRF is the risk, not the implementation.

---

## ADRF-P3 — Cross-System Applicability

An ADRF risk should remain meaningful even when vendor, product, or platform names are removed.

A risk that only applies to a single implementation may be important, but it does not automatically qualify as an ADRF pattern.

---

## ADRF-P4 — Actionable Awareness

An ADRF risk should create meaningful awareness for one or more of the following groups:

- System Builders
- Architects
- Operators
- Governance Bodies
- Compliance Teams
- End Users

The purpose of ADRF is to improve understanding of risk, not merely to document observations.

---

## ADRF-P5 — Maturity Recognition

AI systems are evolving and are expected to contain design risks, limitations, and areas of uncertainty.

ADRF does not assume fault, negligence, or intent.

Its purpose is to identify emerging patterns, encourage constructive discussion, and improve collective understanding of AI system design risks over time.
